1971
The term “groupthink” was first introduced in the November 1971 issue of Psychology Today by psychologist Irving Janis. Janis had conducted extensive research on group decision-making under conditions of stress.
1985
More research surrounding the topic of "groupthink" was conducted and the causes were better understood. Groupthink, mode of thinking in which individual members of small cohesive groups tend to accept a viewpoint or conclusion that represents a perceived group consensus, whether or not the group members believe it to be valid, correct, or optimal. Groupthink reduces the efficiency of collective problem solving within such groups.
2008
Groupthink was one of the contributing factors accredited to the 2008 financial crisis. Banks and hedge fund manargers saw the market heading towards failure, yet avoided considering that outcome entirely.
2016
Groupthink is largely attributed to political polarization in the United States. This correlates to the rise of social media in the sense that people gravitate towards others with similar viewpoints as them.
2010
"The Great Reset" is the name of a book written by Richard Florida which discussed economic incentives which have driven America to the point of the post financial crash era.
2016
In 2016, the theme of the World Economic Forum talked about "Mastering the Fourth Industrial Revolution". The themes of this conference included automation, artificial intelligence, autonomous vehicles, and more. These themes were not too dissimilar form themes layed out in Richard Florida's book.
2020
"The Great Reset" is the name of the 50th annual meeting of the World Economic Forum. The goal of this meeting was to foster more sustainable ways of rebuilding the economy in the era after COVID-19. Proposed initiatives included a goal of net zero carbon emissions, re-invigoration of science and technology, and generally restructuring the economy to a more sustainable model. Similar themes came up in this conference that did in the 2016 conference.
2021
The Great Reset is now used as a buzzword as well as the diving point of many conspiracy theories. Supporters of QAnon claimed that the "Great Reset" would take place after Joe Biden was sworn into office and many correlate it to the phrase "build back better" which has been a phrase repeatedly used by the Biden administration. It was coined by the United Nations and the framework for this process was outlined in the United Nations' Sendai Framework for Disaster Risk Reduction.
